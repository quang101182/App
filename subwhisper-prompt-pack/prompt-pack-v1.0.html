Category 1 — Transcript Cleanup

Fix raw Whisper output: punctuation, structure, hallucinations, and noise artifacts.

Whisper Deep Cleaner

#1.1
When you have raw Whisper output full of run-on sentences, missing punctuation, and no paragraph structure.
You are a professional transcript editor specializing in Whisper AI output. Clean the following raw transcript according to these rules: PUNCTUATION & CAPITALIZATION: - Add proper sentence-ending punctuation (. ! ?) - Capitalize first word of every sentence and proper nouns - Add commas where natural pauses occur in speech - Remove duplicate punctuation (e.g., "??" → "?") STRUCTURE: - Break into paragraphs every 4–8 sentences, or when topic shifts - Do NOT merge content from different speakers into one paragraph - Remove line-break artifacts (single orphaned words on their own line) CONTENT RULES: - Do NOT paraphrase, summarize, or reword content - Do NOT add words that were not spoken - Do NOT remove content unless it is clearly a transcription artifact - Preserve all proper nouns exactly as written Language of transcript: [LANGUAGE] RAW TRANSCRIPT: [PASTE TRANSCRIPT HERE]

Example

Before
so um yeah the the product launch went really well i think we sold like three hundred units in the first day which is amazing and then sarah was saying that the website traffic spiked too like something like forty percent increase
After
The product launch went really well. We sold around three hundred units in the first day, which is amazing. Sarah mentioned that website traffic spiked too — something like a forty percent increase.

Generic closing phrases: "Thanks for watching", "Like and subscribe", "See you next time" 3. Sudden language switch in monolingual transcript 4. TRANSCRIPT: [PASTE TRANSCRIPT HERE]

BONUS 2 — Whisper Troubleshooting Guide

1. Hallucinations

Whisper invents words during silence. Fix: --no_speech_threshold 0.7-0.8, --condition_on_previous_text False, trim silence with FFmpeg, use large-v3.

2. Wrong Language Detected

Auto-detection uses first 30s only. Fix: always specify --language explicitly. For code-switching: split audio by language.

3. Poor Timestamps

Fix: --word_timestamps True, re-encode to WAV 16kHz mono, split files over 2h into 30-45min chunks.

4. Technical Terms Misrecognized

Fix: use --initial_prompt with a paragraph containing correct terms naturally. Build find/replace dictionary. Use Prompt #10.1.

5. Repeated Text / Loops

Fix: --condition_on_previous_text False, --no_speech_threshold 0.8, trim trailing silence, use faster-whisper.

6. Filler Words Over/Under-Removed

Whisper is inconsistent — handle in post-processing, not at transcription. Use Prompt #3.1 or #3.2.

7. No Speaker Diarization

Whisper doesn't natively diarize. Use WhisperX or pyannote.audio separately, then merge. Post-hoc: use Prompt #2.1.

8. Long Files Degrade

Split into 30-min chunks with FFmpeg: ffmpeg -i input.wav -f segment -segment_time 1800 -c copy chunk_%03d.wav

9. Missing Punctuation

tiny/base models have poor punctuation. Use medium+. Apply LLM post-processing (Prompt #1.1).

10. Low Audio Quality

Pre-process: ffmpeg -i input.wav -af "highpass=f=200, lowpass=f=3000, afftdn=nf=-25" output.wav. Accept that SNR <10dB = <90% accuracy.

BONUS 3 — 10 Prompt Engineering Tips for Transcripts

1. Assign a Specific Role

"You are a senior broadcast subtitler" activates better knowledge than "Fix this transcript."

2. Use Clear Delimiters

Separate instructions from transcript with ---TRANSCRIPT START--- to prevent confusion.

3. Specify Output Format First

Tell the model the format before the content — it produces cleaner, more consistent output.

4. Flag Uncertainty Explicitly

Add: "Mark uncertain words with [VERIFY: reason] rather than guessing." Prevents confident hallucination.

5. Chunk Large Transcripts

Split over 10,000 words into chunks. Carry forward last 200 words as context. Quality is measurably better.

6. Use Temperature 0 for Accuracy

Temp 0 = conservative, faithful. Temp 0.7+ = creative rewrites. Use 0 for cleaning, 0.7+ for titles/hooks.

7. Show Examples (Few-Shot)

One before/after example teaches style better than a paragraph of rules.

8. Say What NOT to Change

"Do NOT paraphrase or improve the speaker's language" prevents over-helpful rewriting.

9. Request a Change Log

"Provide a table: Original | Corrected | Reason" — makes the model more careful and gives you an audit trail.

10. Two-Pass: Critique Then Fix

Pass 1: "List every issue. Don't fix." Pass 2: "Fix only the listed issues." Consistently outperforms single-pass.
